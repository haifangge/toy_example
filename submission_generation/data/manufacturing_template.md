# Outline of typical structure of a mid market broking submission

## Intro page
* Broker name and logo
* Broker contact details
* Broking submission for ABC manufacturing Limited
* Date of submission
* Renewal date

Invent a realistc broker name and contact, and a realistic name for insurred company. 

## Risk overview

Introductory couple of paragraphs explaning how this is a well managed risk, long established and long time partner of the broker, why the risk is being marketed. Typically including the following contents: 

* Business description and activities
* Date business establised
* ERN number
* Holding insurer
* Target premium
* Renewal date

## Products and covers required

Section which lists the covers required, for example, including the following contents: 

* Property damage
* Business interruption
* Goods in transit
* Specified all risks
* Terrorism
* Employers liability
* Public and products liability
* Directors and officers

Often this will list out the limits, e.g., PD - All Risks, EL at £10m LOI, PL/Pr at £5m LOI etc. 

## Risk management

This will tell a story of the management of the business, including the contents like: 

* The business has a full-time risk manager
* The business has ISO certifications
* Commitment to environmental management
* Long-standing contracts with major UK auto suppliers

## Property damage cover
There should be 3 to 10 locations. For each location including locations, fire protection and security, as examples given below.

*  Location details
    * Address
    * Contruction type: e.g., Steel frame, insulated composite panel walls, concerete floor, pitched steel roof etc. 
    * Year built: e.g., 2015
    * Floor area: e.g. 2,500 sq meters
    * Occupancy: e.g., 100% manufacturing and storage of finished goods etc. 

* Fire protection
    * Sprinker system: e.g., automatic sprinkler system (NS EN 12845 compliant)
    * Smoke detection system throughout
    * Fire extinguishers serviced annually

* Security
    * Monitored intruder alarm (NSI Gold)
    * CCTV covering all access points
    * Perimeter fencing and gated access
    
### Sums Insured
Sums Insured are usually provided in a table for all locations. Below is a single location example:

* Buildings: £3,200,000 (Day One uplift 15%)
* Machinery & Plant: £1,500,000
* Stock (Raw materials): £400,000
* Stock (Finished goods): £600,000
* Business interruption (Gross profit): £2,000,000 for 12 months indemnity period

### Loss history (past 5 years)
As with Sums Insured, it's quite common for claims to be listed in a table. For example:

* 2019: Minor water ingress claim - £4,200 (fully reparied, no recurrence)
* No other claims

## Risk management and improvements 
An example of contents includes:

* Annual fire risk assessment by accredited consultant 
* Staff fire safety training every 6 months 
* Preventive maintenance schedule for all machinery 
* Waste management: e.g., waste metal stored externally in locked skips away from buildings 

## Liability covers
### Employers liability 

This is a description of the different types of activities that the employees undertake, the risk management in place (e.g., H&S, guarding on machinery etc.). 

It includes a breakdown of the different types of activity and the associated wageroll, e.g., clerical, manual work away, manufacturing, sales etc. 

### Public and and products liability 
This is a description of the nature of the products, and the typical end customers, plus any quality standards the product complies with; where goods or parts are sourced from; any H&S relating to control of access to sites 

Turnover breakdown by territory: UK, EEA, US and Rest of World. 

### Claims
Listing and details of any EL and PL/Pr claims in the last 5 years.
